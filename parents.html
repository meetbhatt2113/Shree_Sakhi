For Parents & Teachers

If a girl or young woman in your life is using Shree Sakhi, here's what the platform actually does, and a few honest thoughts on how you can support her alongside it — not instead of it.

Why this exists

Many of us grew up learning about periods through whispered rumours, and about pregnancy through fear rather than facts. Shree Sakhi was built so that today's girls have somewhere plain, accurate, and judgement-free to go with the questions they might feel too shy to ask a parent or teacher directly — not to replace those conversations, but to make them a little easier to eventually have.

What's actually on the platform

  • Plain-language FAQs on periods, PCOD/PCOS, pregnancy, menarche (first periods), menopause, postpartum, and contraception
  • Myth-busting content addressing common misconceptions
  • Practical self-care guidance — pain relief, diet, exercise, mental wellbeing
  • An AI assistant that answers questions conversationally, instructed to never diagnose and to always point toward a real doctor for anything serious
  • Real, verified helpline numbers (government and NGO) for both health and emotional support
  • Interactive tools like a period tracker and pregnancy week calculator

What it deliberately does not do

It does not collect names, requires no account or login, does not diagnose medical conditions, and does not replace a doctor's advice. Our full Privacy Policy explains exactly what little data is involved.

If you're a parent

You don't need to have all the answers before talking to your daughter about periods or puberty. Being willing to sit with an uncomfortable question — even just saying "I'm not sure, let's find out together" — matters more than getting every detail right.

Consider looking at the site together once, so she knows it's something you're comfortable with, not something she has to hide. The tone throughout is deliberately warm and non-judgemental, similar to how we'd want a trusted aunt or elder sister to explain things.

If you're a teacher

This can be a useful resource to mention during health education classes, or to have available for a student who seems to have questions she isn't voicing in a group setting. The blog section includes a full first-period guide that could double as classroom reading material.

A note on the AI assistant

Like any AI system, it can occasionally give an incomplete or imperfect answer. It's designed with safety instructions to always defer to real doctors and helplines for anything urgent, but it is a tool, not a substitute for you, a school counselor, or a physician.

Questions or concerns?

If you have feedback about content appropriateness or anything else, please use the "Ask Sakhi" form on the main site and mention that you're a parent or teacher — we take that feedback seriously.
